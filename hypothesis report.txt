STATISTICAL HYPOTHESIS TEST RESULTS

1️⃣ One-Sample T-Test
Tested whether average Total_Sales is greater than ₹100,000.

H0: Mean = 100000
H1: Mean > 100000

Result: p-value < 0.05
Decision: Reject H0
Conclusion: Average sales are significantly greater than ₹100,000.

2️⃣ Independent T-Test (North vs South)

H0: Mean North Sales = Mean South Sales
H1: Means are different

Result: p-value < 0.05
Decision: Reject H0
Conclusion: Sales differ significantly between North and South regions.

3️⃣ One-Way ANOVA (All Regions)

H0: All regional means are equal
H1: At least one region differs

Result: p-value < 0.05
Decision: Reject H0
Conclusion: At least one region shows significantly different sales.

Overall Interpretation:
The statistical tests confirm that sales performance is significantly influenced by regional differences and that average sales exceed the benchmark value.
