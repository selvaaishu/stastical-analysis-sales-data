1️⃣ Introduction

This project analyzes business sales data to extract meaningful statistical insights. The objective is to evaluate sales performance, examine relationships between variables, conduct hypothesis testing, and provide actionable business recommendations.

The dataset includes:

Date

Product

Quantity

Price

Customer

Region

Total_Sales

Where:

Total_Sales = Quantity × Price

2️⃣ Methodology

The following statistical techniques were applied:

Descriptive Statistics

Distribution Analysis

Correlation Analysis

Hypothesis Testing (3 tests)

Confidence Interval Estimation

Linear Regression Analysis

All analysis was performed using Python with Pandas, NumPy, SciPy, and Scikit-learn.

3️⃣ Descriptive Statistics

Key metrics calculated for Total_Sales:

Mean (Average Sales)

Median

Mode

Standard Deviation

Variance

📌 Interpretation:

The mean provides average business performance.
Standard deviation measures sales variability.
Higher deviation indicates inconsistent sales patterns.

4️⃣ Distribution Analysis

Histogram and density plots were created to understand sales distribution.

A Shapiro-Wilk test was conducted to test normality.

If p > 0.05 → Sales follow normal distribution.
If p < 0.05 → Sales are not normally distributed.

Understanding distribution helps determine which statistical tests are appropriate.

5️⃣ Correlation Analysis

Pearson correlation was calculated between:

Quantity & Total_Sales

Price & Total_Sales

Quantity & Price

📊 Example Interpretation:

Correlation > 0.7 → Strong positive relationship

Correlation 0.3–0.7 → Moderate relationship

Correlation < 0.3 → Weak relationship

Findings indicate that Quantity has a strong positive correlation with Total_Sales, meaning higher quantities significantly increase sales.

6️⃣ Hypothesis Testing

Three hypothesis tests were conducted:

✅ Test 1: One-Sample T-Test

Research Question:
Is average Total_Sales significantly greater than ₹100,000?

H0: Mean Sales = ₹100,000
H1: Mean Sales > ₹100,000

Result:
p-value < 0.05

Conclusion:
Reject H0.
Average sales are significantly greater than ₹100,000.

✅ Test 2: Independent T-Test (North vs South)

Research Question:
Do North and South regions differ significantly in sales?

H0: Mean North Sales = Mean South Sales
H1: Means are different

Result:
p-value < 0.05

Conclusion:
Reject H0.
Sales differ significantly between regions.

✅ Test 3: ANOVA (All Regions)

Research Question:
Is there a significant difference among all regions?

H0: All regional means are equal
H1: At least one region differs

Result:
p-value < 0.05

Conclusion:
Reject H0.
At least one region has significantly different sales.

7️⃣ Confidence Interval

A 95% confidence interval was calculated for Total_Sales.

Example Format:

Average Sales: ₹150,000 ± ₹12,000 (95% CI)

This means we are 95% confident that the true population mean lies within this range.

Margin of Error indicates reliability of estimation.

8️⃣ Regression Analysis

A linear regression model was built:

Dependent Variable: Total_Sales
Independent Variables: Quantity, Price

Results:

R² ≈ 0.99

Strong predictive relationship

Interpretation:

Sales are highly predictable using Quantity and Price.
Quantity has a direct positive impact on sales.

9️⃣ Business Insights

1️⃣ Higher quantities significantly increase total revenue.
2️⃣ Regional sales differences indicate market imbalance.
3️⃣ Marketing strategies can be customized region-wise.
4️⃣ Sales forecasting is reliable due to strong regression fit.
